About Me

I'm Jeffrey Woods, a software developer and current student at Utah Valley University, obtaining graduating with a BS in Computer Science in April 2025.

My particular fascination within the field of computer science lies within high-performance computing. It all started one day as I was searching for the worst video games in history, and the 1982 version of Pac-Man for the Atari 2600 was mentioned. As I dug deeper into the reasons why the game was so awful, I discovered that the game's creator was given severe hardware limitations relative to the original arcade version of Pac-Man. It was nothing short of a miracle that he was able to recreate the game at all with just 128 bytes of RAM.

I began diving into the subjects of assembly language, utilization of hardware for increased performance, and how understanding a computer's architecture can help you create better software. Upon graduation, I hope to work in the field of low-latency software, such as HFT (high-frequency trading). When I'm not tinkering on a computer, I like fixing and building up cars, and occasionally re-sharpening my Spanish language skills.
